Fatigue: Find the Cause First

“Low energy” is a symptom, not a diagnosis — and it has a long list of common, treatable causes: poor or insufficient sleep, thyroid dysfunction, iron or B12 deficiency, low testosterone, blood-sugar issues, depression, medication effects, and more. Chasing energy with a peptide before ruling these out is backwards.

So the honest starting point isn’t a compound — it’s a work-up. Only once the fixable causes are addressed does it make sense to ask whether a peptide adds anything.

No peptide is a proven treatment for fatigue. The biggest wins usually come from correcting sleep, hormones, iron, thyroid, and metabolic health — not from an injection.

MOTS-c: The Mitochondrial Peptide

The peptide most tied to energy at the cellular level is MOTS-c, a mitochondrial-derived peptide encoded within the mitochondrial genome itself. Mitochondria are your cells’ power plants, so a signal that comes from them is a natural focus for energy metabolism.

MOTS-c’s best-studied action is activating AMP-activated protein kinase (AMPK), the cell’s central energy sensor. Switching on AMPK shifts cells toward taking up glucose and burning fat more efficiently — broadly the same pathway engaged by exercise, which is why MOTS-c is often described as an “exercise-mimetic.”[1][2]

How Strong Is the Evidence?

We’re candid about the gap here. The bulk of MOTS-c evidence comes from cell and animal studies, where it improves insulin sensitivity, protects against diet-induced obesity, and supports metabolic flexibility, with AMPK activation as the recurring mechanism.[1][3]

Human data are far more limited. Circulating MOTS-c rises with exercise and differs across metabolic states, which supports its biological relevance — but there are no large randomized trials showing that administering MOTS-c produces meaningful energy or clinical benefit in people.[2]

MOTS-c is mechanistically compelling with genuinely thin human efficacy data. It is not a substitute for training, sleep, and nutrition — at most it may support the metabolic adaptations those produce.

Other Peptides in the Energy Conversation

A couple of adjacent areas come up:

  • •GH secretagogues and longevity peptides — discussed for vitality and body composition, though “energy” benefits are indirect and individual.

How Strong Health Approaches Energy

We start with the work-up: a history and targeted labs — which can include thyroid function, ferritin/iron studies, B12, testosterone, fasting glucose and metabolic markers — so we’re treating a cause, not a symptom. Fixing an iron deficiency or a sleep disorder beats any peptide.

Where MOTS-c is considered, it’s physician-prescribed, dispensed through licensed 503A/503B compounding pharmacies, and monitored with relevant metabolic labs — framed honestly as an adjunct with early evidence, not a proven fix.

Safety & Side Effects

MOTS-c has a limited human safety record; in preclinical work it has generally been well tolerated, and reported effects in clinical use tend to be mild — most often injection-site irritation. Because long-term human safety data don’t yet exist, we screen carefully and monitor throughout a cycle.
